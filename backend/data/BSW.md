### Bachelor of Social Work (BSW)

The Bachelor of Social Work (BSW) program at Samriddhi College, affiliated with Tribhuvan University, offers a comprehensive foundation in social work. Emphasizing a multidisciplinary approach, the course integrates theories from various social sciences while upholding core social work and psychology values and principles. This holistic framework ensures students receive a well-rounded education that combines rigorous academic learning with practical fieldwork to address the pressing social issues of our time, both locally and globally.At Samriddhi College, the BSW program focuses two majors: Social Work and Psychology for experiential learning through extensive fieldwork, impactful internships, and global collaborations. Students engage in workshops, mock interviews, and pre-placement sessions to develop analytical skills, professional approaches, and compassion for social work. Guided by a diverse and knowledgeable faculty, students benefit from global perspectives and top-notch instruction. The program's success is reflected in the achievements of its graduates, who secure excellent placement opportunities and make meaningful contributions to society.

## BSW and Field Work

Fieldwork is an important part of the social work program. The students are expected to undergo a minimum of 12 hours per week of practical fieldwork. The students are evaluated based on their Midterm assessment, assignments, attendance, application of professional skills in the field, and reflection. Our students will also have opportunities for internships and work at many partner NGOs and INGOs. Students will have camps, guest sessions, field visits, and life skill training as per the needs of the course.

## eligibility criteria

- Students seeking admission in Social Work must have secured at least a D+ in all subjects in Grade 11 and Grade 12\.
- Passed \+ 2 or Intermediate or PCL from TU or from any other institution recognized by TU with a minimum of 45% marks.
- \+2 or equivalent level of education from any board/ institutions/ universities recognized by TU.
- Only those students who have passed the interview will be eligible for admission.

## Career after BSW

# Social and Community Development Sector

- Community Development Officer – Working with NGOs/INGOs for rural and urban development projects.

- Community Mobilizer / Social Mobilizer – Engaging with local communities to raise awareness and implement development programs.

- Community Health Worker – Supporting public health and sanitation awareness campaigns.

# Non-Governmental and International Organizations

Many NGOs/INGOs in Nepal hire BSW graduates for roles like:

- Program Coordinator / Field Officer – Project implementation in education, health, and women empowerment.

- Research Assistant / Monitoring & Evaluation (M\&E) Officer – Assisting in project assessment and reporting.

- Child Protection Officer – Working in child rights and welfare organizations (e.g., UNICEF, Save the Children).

# Social Welfare and Humanitarian Work

- Social Worker – Direct involvement with marginalized communities, orphans, elderly care, and rehabilitation centers.

- Disaster Relief and Humanitarian Aid Worker – Working with disaster response and recovery teams.

# Government and Policy Sector

- Social Development Officer in local government offices (Municipality/Rural Municipality).

- Youth and Women Empowerment Programs – Roles in government-led community development and empowerment schemes.

# Health and Counseling Sector

- Rehabilitation Counselor – In addiction recovery centers.

- Mental Health Counselor (with further specialization or training).

- HIV/AIDS or Public Health Outreach Worker.

# Education and Research

- Research Assistant – In universities, research centers, or policy think tanks.

- Teaching – After completing a Master’s degree (MSW or related fields), you can become a lecturer in colleges.

- Trainer / Facilitator – Conducting workshops on community development, health, or rights awareness.

# Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)

- CSR Officer – Companies and banks now hire BSW graduates to handle their social responsibility programs.

# Entrepreneurship & Advocacy

- Start your own NGO, social enterprise, or community initiative focusing on education, women empowerment, or sustainable development.

- Work as an advocacy specialist for social justice, environment, or human rights.

# Higher Study Options (For Better Career Growth)

- Master in Social Work (MSW)

- Master in Public Health (MPH)

- MA in Sociology / Rural Development / Gender Studies

- Psychology or Counseling specialization
